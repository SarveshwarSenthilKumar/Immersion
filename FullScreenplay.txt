"You know this film is my first foray into filmmaking and directing, and I thought it was a really intriguing plot to explore because it's something that's very relevant to today's world and stands to be a metaphor for various things like change, but it makes the process interesting, or at least I hope it does, and that is the motive to use metaphor right, to help understand in a way that is more effective, in a way that is more entertaining."

Grade 12 English changed me, introduced me to the idea of storytelling as well as this theme of people letting ambition get the best of them and it is something that I have seen in some of my favorite works like Frankenstein, Oppenheimer, as well as Jurassic Park. I think Jurassic Park is one of the best movies of all time cause it serves as a thrilling yet profound tale about the consequences of unchecked ambition and the dangers of playing god with so many complex themes and commentary which still stands today about the dangers of technology and the famous Jeff Goldblum quote that goes "Your scientists were so preoccupied with whether or not they could, that they didn't stop to think if they should", I can reveal the pages of notes i took while watching it and analyzing it for the film, the thing about Jurassic Park is that it could be a horror film with the tension and tone that it encompasses right, but it doesnt, and I think that's something to reflect upon. I remember reading Stephen King's perspective on Why We Crave Horror Movies and while I do think that it can act as a release and be entertaining, I think that it is overshadowed by the true fact which is that the best movies, in my opinion, and the box office to an extent, are the ones which examine the detriments of human behavior to the extreme, and this is proven by the likes of Obsession and Backrooms which are blazing the box office as we speak right, they're not just random jumpscarey gory content, they're well written and well made films which examine various themes like the ideas of love, loss, and grief, and how they can drive people to the edge of sanity like Pearl with ambition and The Shining with loneliness, isolation and so many other great examples.

I could go on and on about my inspirations with film and what my movie: Immersion, actually encompasses but I will let the film speak for itself. See it in theatres this July! and in case I don't see ya, good afternoon, good evening, and good night!
